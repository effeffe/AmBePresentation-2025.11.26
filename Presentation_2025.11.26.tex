\section*{Primary Generator}
\begin{frame}\frametitle{Implementation}
%For this reason, we want to make a simulation that is as accurate as possible, efficient, is comprehensive of the reactions occouring and is open-source, so that it can be used for a plethora of applications. We chose to directly simulate the neutron and carbon-ion from the fast process, which makes simulations a lot faster considering an emission of 2.27E6 fast neutron per curie per second. This way, we spare about 16300 null events per emerging neutron. We implement this via rejection sampling techniques, adopting integrated cross-sections and differential cross sections in the form of Legendre polynomials from Geiger and van Der Zwan data from 1970 to 1976.
Aim: make the simulation as accurate as possible, efficient, open-source, complete
\begin{itemize}
	\item Simulate $n$ and $^{12}\text{C}$ directly\\
	High activity sources $\Rightarrow$ $2.27\times10^6$ fast neutrons/s/Ci\\
	Simulate one fast neutron per event vs one neutron every $\approx~16300$ events using $\alpha$ decay method
	\item Rejection sampling technique
	\item Integrated cross-section (1970) and Legendre polynomials for differentials (1975,1976 Geiger and Van Der Zwan) for fast neutron reaction
\end{itemize}
\end{frame}

\begin{frame}\frametitle{Disadvantages}
%There are nevertheless some disadvantages related to the cross sections, in particular differential cross sections, not being available for beryllium breakup. For this reason, we are ignoring any (a,a') and (a,n) reactions, as we are not simulating any alphas for geant4 to transport. Similarly, we are also lacking the angular decay information for 9Be neutron decaying, as well as cross sections for the 9Be to 5He process. All that we simulate is neutrons, hence any break-up neutrons we are going to have are due to (n,2n) reactions.
\begin{minipage}{\textwidth}
	Differential and Integrated cross section of beryllium-9 break-up not available
	\begin{itemize}
		\item $^{9}\text{Be}(\alpha,\alpha')$ scattering
		\item $^{9}\text{Be}^*$ angular decay information
		\item Other break-up channels more suppressed at interaction energy ($<5$~MeV)\\e.g. $^{9}\text{Be}^* \rightarrow\alpha+ ^{5}\text{He}$
	\end{itemize}
Only have $(n,2n)$ break-up reactions

\section*{Conclusion}
\begin{frame}\frametitle{Conclusion}
\begin{itemize}
	\item Validated AmBe neutron spectrum in Geant4 - Open Source simulation available
	\item Correctly reproduced AmBe signature peaks
	\item Implemented 1970 and 1975-1976 Geiger-Van Der Zwan Cross sections (not otherwise present in Geant)
	\item Faster execution than full $^{241}\text{Am}$ $\alpha$-decay chain recreation
	\item Useful for analysis of flux, neutron moderation and dose in various media
	\item Future analysis of neutron moderation in water bath
\end{itemize}

		\begin{itemize}
